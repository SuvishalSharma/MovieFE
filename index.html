Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire is a Telugu-language epic action thriller directed by Prashanth Neel, starring Prabhas as Devaratha “Deva” Raisaar and Prithviraj Sukumaran as Vardha Raja Mannar. The film is set in the dystopian city-state of Khansaar, where monarchy prevails. In 1985, Deva and Vardha share a close bond, but a violent attack on Deva and his mother forces them into exile. Vardha sacrifices his territory to save Deva’s life, establishing a debt of loyalty that Deva vows to honor. The story spans multiple timelines, blending past tribal conflicts with present-day political power struggles. Years later, Deva is living a quiet life with his mother when he is drawn back into Khansaar by Vardha’s call for help. A series of events involving kidnappings, political conspiracies, and clashes with ruthless mercenaries force Deva to confront powerful enemies. He rescues Aadhya, the daughter of an NRI, and faces opposition from both the ruling Mannar family and rival tribes. During the conflict, Deva proves his strength and loyalty, taking down powerful foes, including Ghaniyaar and Mannar allies who threaten Vardha’s claim to leadership. The film delves into Khansaar’s history, revealing tribal wars, betrayals, and the rise of Raja Mannar, who exterminates the Shouryaanga tribe to secure his dominance. Deva is eventually revealed as the surviving heir of the Shouryaanga tribe and rightful successor to Khansaar’s throne. Vardha, despite losing territory, recognizes Deva’s power and appoints him as the “Salaar,” cementing his position as a formidable warrior and protector. The film balances intense action sequences with political drama, showcasing both personal loyalty and large-scale tribal conflicts. Released on 22 December 2023, Salaar: Part 1 – Ceasefire was praised for its high-octane action, grand production design, and the performances of Prabhas and Prithviraj. Music by Ravi Basrur and cinematography by Bhuvan Gowda contributed to its immersive experience. The film became a commercial success, earning over ₹614–702 crore worldwide. With a sequel, Salaar: Part 2 – Shouryaanga Parvam, already announced for 2026, the story promises to explore the next chapter of Deva’s rise and the continuing power struggles in Khansaar.

About Director

Prashanth Neel began his journey in the film industry with a clear vision to make action-packed, story-driven cinema. He made his directorial debut with “Ugramm” (2014), which was a critical and commercial success, earning him recognition as a filmmaker capable of crafting intense narratives with strong emotional depth. His style is marked by gritty realism combined with larger-than-life action sequences that captivate audiences. After Ugramm, Neel’s career skyrocketed with the KGF series, which redefined Kannada cinema on a national and international level. The films are noted for their epic scale, powerful dialogues, and iconic character arcs,particularly the rise of Rocky, which became a cultural phenomenon. His ability to balance raw action with emotional storytelling has made him a favorite among both critics and mass audiences. Prashanth Neel is also known for his hands-on approach to filmmaking. He involves himself deeply in scripting, casting, and even the choreography of action sequences. His collaborations with top technicians, including cinematographers and music directors like Ravi Basrur, have resulted in visually striking and musically memorable films. With Salaar, he continues to push the boundaries of action cinema, cementing his place as one of India’s most promising and visionary directors.

About Music Director



Ravi Basrur is a talented Indian music composer, singer, and sound designer, primarily known for his work in Kannada cinema. He gained widespread recognition with his music in “Ugramm” (2014), where his background score and songs perfectly complemented the film’s intense narrative and gritty action sequences. His compositions are known for blending raw energy with melodic depth, creating a lasting impact on audiences. He rose to national fame with the KGF series, where his music played a crucial role in amplifying the epic scale and dramatic tension of the films. Tracks like the theme music of Rocky became iconic, with powerful rhythms and orchestration that enhanced the film’s grandeur. Ravi Basrur’s ability to match music to the emotional and visual intensity of the story sets him apart from many composers in the industry. Apart from films, Ravi is also celebrated for his skill in sound design, ensuring that every punch, explosion, and dialogue hits the audience with maximum effect. His collaborations with director Prashanth Neel have been particularly successful, as they share a vision for cinematic storytelling where music and visuals work seamlessly together. With Salaar, he continues to push creative boundaries, delivering music that is both larger-than-life and emotionally resonant.
